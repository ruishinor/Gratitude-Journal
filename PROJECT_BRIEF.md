# Project Brief: The Gratitude Journal

## Project Overview

The Gratitude Journal is a web-based platform designed to help users cultivate gratitude through structured reflection. The site presents users with thoughtfully crafted prompts that encourage positive self-reflection, offering a moment of calm in daily life and helping individuals shift their focus from what's lacking to what's present.

## Core Purpose

The platform exists to:

- Provide an easily accessible space for gratitude practice
- Help users reduce stress and break negative thought spirals
- Create moments for positive reflection
- Build a community where shared experiences can inspire others
- Support mental wellbeing through guided journaling

## Primary Features

### Prompt System
- Displays curated gratitude prompts to users
- Allows users to get new random prompts
- Each prompt is designed to shift focus toward positive aspects of life

### Reflection Submission
- Users can write and submit their reflections on selected prompts
- Submissions are reviewed before potential publication
- A reflection form captures user responses along with the prompt that inspired them

### Community Gallery
- A collection of shared reflections from community members
- Provides inspiration and connection through shared experiences
- Shows others' gratitude reflections in a curated format

### Feedback Mechanism
- Anonymous feedback form for users to share their experience
- Captures user sentiment about the platform
- Helps improve the service based on user input

### About Section
- Explains the purpose and research behind gratitude journaling
- Describes how the prompts help shift mindset
- Provides context for why the space exists
- Includes information about the creator (Holf Services / RuishiNOR)

### Utility Features
- Share functionality allowing users to spread awareness
- Support link for those who wish to contribute financially
- Statistics tracking (visitor count, prompts shown)
- Year display for copyright purposes

## Target Audience

- Individuals seeking a simple, low-barrier gratitude practice
- People experiencing stress or negative thought patterns
- Those interested in mental wellness and self-reflection
- Anyone looking for a quick moment of positive reflection

## Content Source

The 90 prompts in the collection are sourced from "The Complete Gratitude Workbook" by RuishiNOR, which contains additional resources for deeper gratitude practice.

## Business Context

The website is published by Holf Services under the RuishiNOR brand. It offers a free service to users while providing an option for supporters to contribute financially through a support link.

## Goals

1. Provide immediate value to users seeking a moment of reflection
2. Build an engaged community through shared reflections
3. Make gratitude practice accessible to anyone with internet access
4. Support user wellbeing through regular positive reflection
5. Grow reach through social sharing and organic discovery

## Non-Goals (Out of Scope)

This brief intentionally avoids specifying:

- Visual design elements or styling choices
- Color palettes, typography, or imagery
- Implementation technologies or frameworks
- Specific layout arrangements or UI components

These aspects will be determined through the design and development process to allow creative exploration without bias.
